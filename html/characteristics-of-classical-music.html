From Classicism to Romanticism

Characteristics of Classical music

From ornamented Baroque to simple Clasicism

Classical music seeks balance, clarity and proportion. It moves away from the expressive excess of the Baroque period and focuses on elegant, ordered and easy-to-understand music.

Melodies are simple, regular and symmetrical, organised into clear phrases. Melody with accompaniment predominates, with a main melodic line clearly standing out over a simple harmonic accompaniment.

Musical forms are very well structured and clearly defined, especially the “sonata form”, which became the basis of much instrumental music.
