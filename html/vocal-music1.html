From Classicism to Romanticism

Vocal Music

The intimist Lied and the epic opera

During Romanticism, vocal music experienced great development.

The lied

The lied stands out especially: a short song for voice and piano based on a poem, in which text and music merge to express feelings. The main composer of lieder was Franz Schubert.

Listening:  Ellen’s Third Song “Ave Maria.” by Franz Schubert.

Opera

Romantic opera reached its peak. In Italy (G. Verdi), bel canto triumphed and later verismo (G. Puccini). In Germany, Richard Wagner stands out as the creator of musical drama and the use of the leitmotif.

  • Escuchamos: Libiamo ne'lieti calici (Brindis) from la Traviata by Verdi.
  • Escuchamos: Nessun dorma (aria) from Turandot by Puccini.
  • Escuchamos: Ride of the Valkyries (prelude) from Die Walküre by R. Wagner.
