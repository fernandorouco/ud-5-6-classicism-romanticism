From Classicism to Romanticism

Historical Context

Music for the people

Musical Classicism developed approximately between 1750 (death of J. S. Bach) and 1827 (death of Beethoven), during the period known as the Enlightenment. This era was marked by confidence in reason, progress and education. The bourgeoisie grew socially and economically and became the new audience for music.

This caused an important change: music stopped being exclusive to the Church and noble courts and began to spread through private salons and public concerts. In bourgeois (burguesía) homes, interest in learning to play instruments increased, which led to the growth of printed scores and music instruction books.

The composer gradually began to break free from traditional patronage (mecenazgo, protección de un doble). Although commissions and patronage still existed, some composers started writing more for the public and according to their own taste, for a specific noble.
