Women in Romanticism

The first ones with recognition

Although difficulties continued, Romanticism allowed greater visibility for some women in music, especially as performers. A notable example is Clara Schumann, a great pianist and composer. Even so, many women saw their careers limited by the social norms of the time, and their recognition as composers was far less than that of men. Notable figures include:
